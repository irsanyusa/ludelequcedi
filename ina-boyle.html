Ina Boyle (Composer) - On This Day

Publish date: 2024-07-20

Profession: Composer

Biography: The most prolific Irish female composer of the early 20th century, Boyle's work was rarely performed in lifetime. Although she studied in London with Ralph Vaughan Williams, the bulk of her life spent in rural Ireland caring for her family.

Her works won awards and competitions in the 1930s, but fell into obscurity until after her death. Notable pieces include her "Glencree Symphony", "The Magic Harp", and "Elegy".

Born: March 8, 1899
Birthplace: Bushey Park, County Wicklow, Ireland

Generation: Lost Generation
Star Sign: Pisces

Died: March 10, 1967 (aged 68)
Cause of Death: Cancer
