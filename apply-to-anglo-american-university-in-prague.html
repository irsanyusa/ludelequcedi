Apply to Anglo-American University in Prague! - Live &amp; Study

Publish date: 2024-07-12

22. 12. 2020 | Study in the Czech Republic

Anglo-American University (AAU) offers a wide range of undergraduate programs across five different schools as well as in cooperation with international partners. As the first private university in the Czech Republic to use English as a language of instruction, AAU carries both Czech and American accreditation and provides its students a unique learning environment. Submit your application now to begin your studies in the heart of Europe in 2021!

Prospective students can choose from programs in the areas of business, humanities, journalism, visual arts, international relations, diplomacy, and law. The campus, located in the centre of Prague, surrounded by beautiful parks and amidst splendid adjacent historical buildings, is an international hub where academia intermingles with business, diplomatic circles, the media, and the local community.

Anglo-American University accepts applications year round, therefore you can submit your application anytime you’d like! Perspective students can also begin their studies at AAU in either Fall or Spring semester thanks to the flexibility of the study programs. The admission process usually takes less than two weeks from the date you submit your application, but it is recommended to apply as early as possible.

You can enter AAU as a first-year student or if you have credits which are transferable, you can apply as a transferring student. If you wish to apply, you should submit an online application along with the required documents via the AAU Application Portal. The required documents include a high school diploma, high school transcript, CV, a proof of English efficiency, personal statement, a copy of passport and a passport size photo. In the case of transferring students, the transcript must be sent directly from your university in a sealed envelope. To find out more about the application process, go to https://www.aauni.edu/admissions/undergraduate-admission/.
