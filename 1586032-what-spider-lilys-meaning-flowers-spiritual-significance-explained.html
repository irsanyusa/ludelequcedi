What is the spider lily's meaning? The flower's spiritual significance explained

Publish date: 2024-05-30

Spider lily, scientifically known as Lycoris radiata, is a flower native to China, Japan, Korea, and Nepal. It is called 'spider lilies' because its petals resemble spider legs. They have a unique and striking appearance. Aside from their physical beauty, several meanings are associated with the flower. Get to know various spider lily meanings and when to use them.

Spider lily flowers have several other names, such as red magic lily, corpse flower, and equinox flower. They are unique because, aside from their aesthetic purposes, their roots offer medicinal value. They can treat illnesses such as ulcers and swellings.

What does spider lily mean? Merriam-Webster defines it as any of several plants of the genus Nerine. According to Collins Dictionary, spider lily is;

Any plants that have lily-like flowers with narrow petals, such as those belonging to the amaryllis family, such as genera Crinium, Hymenocallis, and Lycoris.

Yes, spider lilies can bloom in different colours, including pink. They symbolise love, beauty, passion, and pride and can also be used to wish someone success.

Yellow and pink spider lilies can symbolise love towards the other partner. So giving these flowers to your partner can be a great way to express your feelings towards them.

Can you touch a red spider lily?

The flower is toxic, so skin contact with its sap can be harmful. People with delicate skin, especially babies, can be affected.
